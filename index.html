Phase 1 of 6 — Foundation

What is your team
responsible for delivering?

Complete this template to understand your team's current state. This forms the foundation for all hiring decisions and will auto-populate later templates. Takes 15–20 minutes.

Q1 of 6
Section A — Team Context · Question 1 of 6
What is your team or function?
Give a short description — team name, size, and company stage. This sets the context for every document.
Section A — Team Context · Question 2 of 6
What is the primary purpose of this team?
Why does this team exist? What commercial or operational value does it create?
💡
Think about the outcome your team delivers — not the tasks they do. e.g. "Build and ship product features that drive commercial growth — specifically the enterprise tier that enables Sales to close deals and achieve £2M ARR by Q4 2026"
Section A — Team Context · Question 3 of 6
Who are your key stakeholders?
Who depends on this team's outcomes? List the people and teams who rely on your output.
💡
Include names, roles, and what they need from your team. This helps map dependencies across your documents.
Sections B & C — Outcomes · Question 4 of 6
What are your team's top outcomes right now?
Select 3–6 outcome groups from the 12 Universal Outcome Groups below. For each, describe your specific version with a measure and timeframe. Number your top 3 in priority order (1, 2, 3).
💡
Ask: what would need to be true in 12 months for this team to have had a great year? Most teams draw on 4–7 of the 12 groups. You don't need all 12.

Phase 2 of 6 — Decision

Do you actually need
to hire someone?

Before committing to a hire, confirm it's the right solution. This generates your Hiring Decision Brief — which you can share with your manager for sign-off. Takes 5–10 minutes.

Q1 of 3
Question 1 — Carried forward from Phase 1
Which outcomes are currently at risk?
↑
Your outcomes and gap analysis from Phase 1 have been carried forward automatically.
Question 2 of 3
What is the root cause of this gap?
Select all that apply, then describe the specifics for each. Be honest — this shapes whether you actually need to hire.
💡
Most gaps come down to one of four causes. Picking the right one changes your solution. You might not need to hire at all.
✓
Capacity Issue — Not enough hours or people
The work exists and the skills exist, but there aren't enough people to do it
✓
Capability Issue — Required skills are missing
The team doesn't have the experience this outcome demands
✓
Ownership Issue — Nobody is accountable
The work exists but no one owns the outcome end to end
✓
Dependency Issue — Blocked waiting on others
Progress is stalled because of another team, decision, or system
